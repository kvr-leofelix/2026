Hello there. It's excellent to see you diving into the mechanics of prompt engineering—it is the syntax of the future. As a senior lead, I often tell my junior devs: **Large Language Models are not magic; they are engines.** If you feed them vague fuel, you get sputtering performance. If you engineer your inputs with precision, you get production-grade output.

Below is the complete breakdown of the 21 prompts discussed in the lecture, categorized by their engineering function. I have extracted the specific "source code" for each prompt and defined its use case so you can implement these immediately.

### **Phase 1: Foundation (The Syntax of Control)**

These prompts correct the most common error: treating the LLM like a search engine instead of a processor.

**1. Basic Structure**

* **The Prompt:** "You are an experienced marketing strategist. Write a 1,000-word blog post about email marketing for small businesses. Include specific tactics, avoid generic advice, and format it with clear subheadings and bullet points." [[00:53](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=53)]
* **Usage:** Use this to move from "Amateur Hour" (generic walls of text) to professional, structured deliverables. It establishes **Role + Task + Constraints + Formatting**.

**2. Roleplaying Power**

* **The Prompt:** "You are Gordon Ramsay teaching cooking to a nervous home chef. I want to make beef Wellington but I've never done pastry work. Be encouraging but honest about the challenges. Give me step-by-step instructions in your characteristic style." [[02:29](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=149)]
* **Usage:** Use this when you need a specific **tone** or **perspective**. By invoking a known persona, you implicitly load a massive set of behavioral weights (vocabulary, attitude, expertise).

**3. Step-by-Step Mastery**

* **The Prompt:** "I want to start an online consulting business. Walk me through this step by step. First help me identify my expertise and target market. Wait for my response before moving to step two. Then we'll work on pricing strategy, then marketing approach, then legal setup. Ask clarifying questions at each step." [[03:27](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=207)]
* **Usage:** Essential for complex workflows. It forces the model to **pause** (the "stop sequence") so it doesn't hallucinate a shallow 500-word answer for a 3-month project.

**4. Formatting Control**

* **The Prompt:** "Create a comparison between iPhone and Android phones. Format your response as a table with these columns: Feature, iPhone, Android, Winner. Include 10 key comparison points. After the table provide a one paragraph recommendation for business users and another for creative professionals." [[04:20](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=260)]
* **Usage:** Use this to generate **data structures** (tables, JSON, CSV) or reports that are ready to copy-paste into presentation slides.

**5. Context and Constraints**

* **The Prompt:** "I'm a freelance web designer who primarily works with local restaurants. I need to write a cold email to a new Italian restaurant that just opened downtown. The email should be personal but professional, mention their recent opening, offer specific web design services they likely need and include a soft call to action. Keep it under 150 words and avoid overly salesy language." [[06:05](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=365)]
* **Usage:** The "Sweet Spot" prompt. It balances **background info** (Context) with **negative constraints** ("avoid overly salesy language") to prevent the model from drifting.

**6. Style Mirroring**

* **The Prompt:** "Analyze the writing style in this email I wrote: [Paste your email]. Now write a follow-up email to the same client about project delays matching my tone, sentence structure and communication approach exactly." [[06:55](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=415)]
* **Usage:** Use this for **consistency**. It essentially "fine-tunes" the model on your specific voice for a single session.

**7. Error Correction**

* **The Prompt:** "That response was too technical for my audience. Rewrite it for business owners who aren't familiar with marketing jargon. Use simple language and practical examples instead of theory." [[07:39](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=459)]
* **Usage:** Debugging the output. Instead of restarting, you guide the model to **refactor** its response.

### **Phase 2: Logic & Reasoning (Advanced Processing)**

Here we move from simple generation to complex cognition.

**8. Chain of Thought Reasoning**

* **The Prompt:** "I need to decide whether to hire a full-time marketing manager or use freelancers for my growing SaaS company. Walk me through your reasoning process step by step. First analyze the pros and cons of each approach. Then consider the specific context of SaaS businesses. Finally factor in cost, scalability and control. Show me your complete thought process before giving your final recommendation." [[09:05](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=545)]
* **Usage:** This is the "Show Your Work" prompt. It forces the model to generate intermediate logic steps, which drastically reduces logic errors and hallucinations.

**9. Nested Complexity**

* **The Prompt:** "Create a content marketing strategy for a B2B software company. Structure your response in three nested levels: Level 1: Overall strategy and goals. Level 2: Monthly themes and content pillars. Level 3: Specific content pieces for month one including blog topics, social media posts and email sequences. For each content piece provide the title, target audience, key points to cover and distribution channels." [[10:05](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=605)]
* **Usage:** For creating **hierarchical systems**. It generates depth and breadth simultaneously.

**10. Perspective Switching**

* **The Prompt:** "I'm considering pivoting my consulting business from general business consulting to AI implementation. Specifically analyze this decision from three perspectives: 1. As a conservative CFO focused on financial risk. 2. As an aggressive growth strategist focused on market opportunity. 3. As a cautious operations manager focused on execution challenges. After all three analyses, synthesize the insights into actionable recommendations." [[10:56](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=656)]
* **Usage:** Use this to simulate a **board meeting**. It forces the model to break out of its neutral "average" bias and explore extremes.

**11. Image Generation Mastery**

* **The Prompt:** "Generate a minimalist logo for a sustainable tech startup. The company develops solar panel optimization software. Style should be clean, geometric shapes, incorporate subtle solar or tech elements. Use a palette of deep blue and gold suitable for both digital and print applications. Avoid cliche sun symbols or overly complex designs. Create something that would work well on business cards and billboards equally." [[11:44](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=704)]
* **Usage:** Precision visual engineering. Note the specific **technical constraints** (palette, application use case) and **negative prompts** ("avoid cliche sun symbols").

**12. Vision Analysis Power**

* **The Prompt:** "I'm uploading a photo of my competitor's retail display. Analyze this image from a marketing perspective. Identify the target demographic they're appealing to, evaluate the effectiveness of product placement, assess the use of color and visual hierarchy and suggest three specific improvements I can implement in my own store layout." [[12:35](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=755)]
* **Usage:** Using the model as a **visual consultant**, extracting semantic meaning from pixels.

**13. OCR and Document Intelligence**

* **The Prompt:** "I'm uploading a screenshot of my competitor's pricing page. First extract all the pricing information accurately. Then analyze their pricing strategy. What market position are they taking? What psychology are they using? Where are the gaps I could exploit? Finally suggest how I should position my pricing to compete effectively." [[13:15](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=795)]
* **Usage:** Competitive Intelligence. It converts raw unstructured image data into **strategic analysis**.

**14. Voice Mode Conversations**

* **The Prompt:** "I want to practice a sales pitch for a new client. You'll play the role of a skeptical business owner who needs convincing. Interrupt me with realistic objections and questions. Let's have a natural conversation where you challenge my points and I respond. Start by asking me what I'm selling." [[13:54](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=834)]
* **Usage:** **Simulation Training**. Great for interview prep or negotiation practice in real-time.

**15. Agent Mode Automation**

* **The Prompt:** "Research and create a comprehensive competitive analysis for my email marketing software startup. Identify the top 10 competitors, analyze their pricing, features and market positioning. Create a comparison matrix of competitors, highlight market gaps and suggest positioning strategies for my product. Work through this systematically and ask me clarifying questions if something is unclear or needs more detail." [[14:48](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=888)]
* **Usage:** Autonomous execution. You are assigning a **project**, not a task.

### **Phase 3: Expert Systems (Senior Architect Level)**

These are the heavy hitters for building systems and handling high-ambiguity problems.

**16. Study Mode Mastery**

* **The Prompt:** "I need to learn digital marketing fundamentals for my business but I'm a visual learner who learns best through practical examples. Create a personalized learning path. Start with a knowledge assessment to identify what I already know. Then create lessons that build systematically, use real business case studies, include practical exercises I can implement and check my understanding before moving to the next concept. Begin with the assessment." [[15:30](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=930)]
* **Usage:** Creating a **personalized tutor** that adapts to *your* learning curve (not a generic syllabus).

**17. Advanced Data Analysis**

* **The Prompt:** "I'm uploading six months of sales data for my e-commerce business. Perform a comprehensive analysis: Identify seasonal trends, calculate customer lifetime value patterns, analyze product performance correlations, detect any anomalies or unusual patterns and predict likely outcomes for the next quarter. Present findings as an executive summary with visual recommendations for strategic decisions." [[16:24](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=984)]
* **Usage:** Turning the model into a **Data Scientist**. It moves beyond "reading" data to *interpreting* it.

**18. Creative Combination Mastery**

* **The Prompt:** "Create a marketing campaign that combines the storytelling approach of Pixar movies with the direct response techniques of old school mail order ads targeted at millennial entrepreneurs who are building their first businesses. The campaign should feel emotionally compelling but drive immediate action. Include three different touch points: an email sequence, social media content and a landing page concept." [[17:10](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=1030)]
* **Usage:** **Innovation Synthesis**. Combining two unrelated concepts (Pixar + Direct Response) to create something novel.

**19. Template Creation Systems**

* **The Prompt:** "Create a comprehensive template system for client onboarding in my web design business. Include: initial client questionnaire with strategic questions, project timeline template that can be customized, communication templates for each project phase and a final delivery checklist. Make each template professional but personalized to my brand voice. Also create instructions for how to customize these templates for different client types." [[17:51](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=1071)]
* **Usage:** **Scalability**. Don't ask for a fish; ask for a fishing trawler design. This builds *assets* you can reuse.

**20. Handling Complex Problems**

* **The Prompt:** "I'm dealing with a complex business situation. My main client wants to reduce their budget by 40% but they also want to expand the scope of work. I have three team members whose jobs might be at risk and I'm considering pivoting to a different service model entirely. Help me think through this systematically. First map out all the interconnected factors and potential consequences. Then generate multiple solution paths. Evaluate each path's risks and benefits. And finally recommend a decision framework I can use to choose the best approach." [[18:33](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=1113)]
* **Usage:** **Strategic Crisis Management**. Use this for ambiguous, high-stakes problems with no single right answer.

**21. Master Integration Prompt**

* **The Prompt:** "I am the founder of a growing digital marketing agency. I need a complete strategic plan that addresses multiple interconnected challenges. Act as my senior business advisor and work through this systematically. Phase 1: Analyze my current situation using the data I'll provide about revenue, team structure and client portfolio... Phase 2: Research market trends... Phase 3: Develop three distinct growth scenarios... Phase 4: Create implementation templates... Phase 5: Build monitoring system... Work through each phase methodically, ask clarifying questions when needed and present deliverables that I can implement immediately. Begin with Phase 1 and wait for my input before proceeding." [[19:24](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBlX6FhDm2E&t=1164)]
* **Usage:** The **Grand Architect**. This combines role-playing, step-by-step logic, and nested complexity to manage an entire business lifecycle.

Study these well. The difference between a junior dev and a senior architect often comes down to who can command the tools to do the heavy lifting. Good luck with your studies.
